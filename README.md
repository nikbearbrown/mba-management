## Overview

**Audience.** This book is written for the undergraduate business student taking their first organizational-behavior course — typically a sophomore or junior in a business, management, or human-resources program. They will become managers, founders, HR partners, consultants, or analysts who depend on workplace research without becoming behavioral scientists themselves. They have heard a lot of management folklore. The book's job is to separate which parts are supported by careful research, which parts are partially supported under specific conditions, and which parts are corporate ritual.

## About the Author

**Nik Bear Brown** teaches at Northeastern University and advises early-stage AI startups through [Bear Brown & Company](https://www.bearbrown.co/). The management questions founders face — when to hire, how to delegate, what to escalate — sit at the center of his consulting practice. He is the founder of [Humanitarians AI](https://www.humanitarians.ai/), a 501(c)(3) he has managed since 2019.

He holds an MBA from Northeastern alongside a PhD in Computer Science from UCLA. He is the author of the *with LLMs* textbook series.
