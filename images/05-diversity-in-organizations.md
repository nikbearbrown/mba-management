# Images and Figures — Chapter 5: Diversity in Organizations

## Figure references in chapter

**[FIGURE: US Labor Force Composition by Race/Ethnicity, 1997 vs. 2016]**
- 1997: White 77%, Hispanic 7%, Black 11%, Asian 4%
- 2016: White 78%, Hispanic 17%, Black 12%, Asian 6%
- Source: US Bureau of Labor Statistics labor force characteristics by race and ethnicity
- Purpose: Show demographic shift in workforce composition over 19 years; illustrate that demographic change outpaced expectations

**[FIGURE: US Labor Force Participation by Gender, 1977 vs. 2017]**
- 1977: Men 59%, Women 41%
- 2017: Men 53%, Women 47%
- Source: US Department of Labor, Bureau of Labor Statistics employment status tables
- Purpose: Illustrate women's growing participation but also its plateauing; show that gender balance in workforce is fact, not strategy

**[FIGURE: Performance of Homogeneous vs. Heterogeneous Teams Over Time (Watson et al.)]**
- X-axis: Weeks (5, 9, 13, 17)
- Y-axis: Overall performance score
- Line 1 (homogeneous): Starts high (week 5), stays level, ends level
- Line 2 (heterogeneous): Starts lower (week 5), rises gradually, ends higher than homogeneous
- Measures tracked: Range of perspectives, number of problems identified, alternatives generated, solution quality
- Purpose: Illustrate the temporal dynamics of diverse team performance; show that homogeneity is efficient early but diverse teams improve over time

**[FIGURE: Three Perspectives on Diversity in Organizations (Ely & Thomas)]**
- Column 1: Integration-and-Learning
  - Belief: Different life experiences and perspectives are valuable resource
  - Approach: Integrate diverse perspectives into decisions and strategy
  - Expected outcome: High creativity, innovation, performance
  - Culture risk: Majority-group members may feel excluded from diversity work
- Column 2: Access-and-Legitimacy
  - Belief: Diversity helps access diverse markets and customers
  - Approach: Hire diverse people to access markets
  - Expected outcome: Moderate performance, moderate retention
  - Culture risk: Minority employees feel valued only instrumentally
- Column 3: Discrimination-and-Fairness
  - Belief: Diversity is moral duty and right thing to do
  - Approach: Equal opportunity, sometimes implicit assimilation
  - Expected outcome: Legal compliance, moderate retention
  - Culture risk: Minority employees feel devalued; assimilation pressure
- Purpose: Distinguish three organizational philosophies and their outcomes

**[FIGURE: Wage Gap by Gender and Education, 2024]**
- Shows median weekly earnings: Bachelor's degree and higher
- Men's median: $1,500/week
- Women's median: $1,200/week
- Gap: 20% (women earn 80 cents per dollar)
- Variations by major, field, time worked
- Source: US Bureau of Labor Statistics current population survey
- Purpose: Illustrate persistence of gender wage gap even with equal education; note that gap exists across all education levels

**[FIGURE: Federal Discrimination Charges by Type (EEOC Recent Data)]**
- Bar chart showing frequency of charges filed with EEOC in past 3 years
- Categories: Race, Sex (including sexual orientation as of 2020), Disability, Age, Religion, National Origin, Retaliation
- Race and sex represent plurality of charges
- Purpose: Show what kinds of discrimination people actually report; retaliation is often overlooked but common

**[FIGURE: Stereotyping and Identity Groups]**
- Boxes or circles for each major demographic group (Black, Hispanic, Asian, Women, LGBTQ+, Older workers, People with disabilities)
- Each contains common stereotypes and research findings that contradict or complicate the stereotype
- Purpose: Make visible the specific stereotypes that operate and their inaccuracy

## Data accessibility notes

All figures use publicly available data from:
- US Census Bureau
- US Bureau of Labor Statistics
- US Equal Employment Opportunity Commission
- Peer-reviewed studies (Watson et al., Ely & Thomas cited in chapter)

Figures are designed to be readable in grayscale and to be accessible to people with color blindness. Labels should include both color and pattern distinction.
