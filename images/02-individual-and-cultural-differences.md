- Static strength (sustained force) — holding a position under load
- Explosive strength (sudden force) — rapid movement tasks

- Arm-hand steadiness (precision under control) — medical procedures
- Eye-hand coordination (visual-motor integration) — piloting, precision work

- Rate control (tracking moving targets) — vehicle operation

**Location in text:** Concept 2, "The Myers-Briggs trap" section.

**Content:**

| Feature | MBTI | Big Five |

## Figure 6: Hofstede's Six Cultural Dimensions

**Location in text:** Concept 3, "Hofstede's six dimensions" section.

**Type:** Dimension spectrum diagram.

**Content:**
Six spectra showing cultural variation:

1. **Power Distance: Low ←→ High**
   - Low: Denmark, Israel, Austria (authority distributed)
   - High: Malaysia, Mexico, India (hierarchy accepted)

2. **Individualism-Collectivism: Individualistic ←→ Collectivist**
   - Individualistic: USA, Australia, UK (personal goals, individual rights)
   - Collectivist: China, Guatemala, Ecuador (group harmony, group identity)

3. **Masculinity-Femininity: Feminine ←→ Masculine**
   - Feminine (low): Cooperation, care, quality of life valued
   - Masculine (high): Achievement, competition, material rewards valued

4. **Uncertainty Avoidance: Low ←→ High**
   - Low: Singapore, Denmark, Jamaica (comfortable with ambiguity)
   - High: Greece, Portugal, Japan (need for rules and structure)

5. **Long-term vs. Short-term Orientation: Short-term ←→ Long-term**
   - Short-term: USA, Australia, Nigeria (quick results, principles)
   - Long-term: China, Hong Kong, Japan (persistence, adaptation)

6. **Indulgence-Restraint: Restrained ←→ Indulgent**
   - Restrained: Pakistan, Egypt, China (self-control, seriousness)
   - Indulgent: Venezuela, Mexico, Australia (gratification, lighter approach)

**Production note:** Can be rendered as six separate spectrum diagrams or as a hexagon with dimensions as axes. Country examples should be visually positioned on each spectrum.

## Figure 7: Cultural Dimension Examples — Four Countries

**Location in text:** Concept 3, "Hofstede's six dimensions" section.

**Type:** Comparative profile diagram.

**Content:**
A visualization comparing four countries (Mexico, Germany, Japan, United States) across the six dimensions. Could be rendered as:
- Four radar/spider charts (one per country)
- A grouped bar chart with countries as groups and dimensions as bars
- A heatmap with countries as rows and dimensions as columns

Typical profiles (from source material):
- **USA**: Low power distance, high individualism, moderate-high masculinity, moderate uncertainty avoidance, short-term orientation, moderate-high indulgence
- **Mexico**: High power distance, moderate-low individualism, high masculinity, high uncertainty avoidance, long-term orientation, high indulgence
- **Japan**: Moderate power distance, low-moderate individualism, high masculinity, high uncertainty avoidance, long-term orientation, low indulgence
- **Germany**: Low power distance, moderate-high individualism, high masculinity, moderate-high uncertainty avoidance, moderate-low long-term orientation, high indulgence

**Production note:** Radar charts work well for within-country profiles. For cross-country comparison, grouped bars or a heatmap is more readable.

## Production workflow notes

### High-priority figures
Figures 3 (Big Five spectrum), 6 (Hofstede dimensions), and 7 (Four-country comparison) are central to understanding the chapter. These should be clear and visually appealing.

### Data source verification
All figure data is sourced from:
- Costa & McCrae (Big Five definitions and predictions)
- Hofstede (1980, 2010) for cultural dimensions
- Laurent (1983) for manager behavior cross-nationally
- Capraro & Capraro (2002) for MBTI reliability data

### Accessibility notes
- Figures 3, 6 should have high contrast for readability
- Color coding can aid learning but should not be the only distinguishing feature (use patterns, labels)
- All spectrum figures should include both visual and verbal labels
- Tables should include alt-text for screen readers

### Integration with text
Each figure is referenced by number in the main chapter. Production should ensure figure numbers are consistent between this guide and final layout.
